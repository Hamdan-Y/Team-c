Maldives

The Maldives which are also known as " the tropical paradise " is not a single island, it consists about 1,200 small coral islands. It is the best place to travel for the people who love's nature. It is situated in the north-central Indian ocean. National currency of Maldives is Rufiya but US dollar is acceptable throughout the market. The capital of Maldives is Male. The only one airport in Maldives is also situated in Male, that is the Valena International airport. One of the worst things about Maldives is it's climate. It remains very hot as it is situated near to the equator. The language spoken in Maldives is Dhivehi and the state religion is Islam. It took around 1hr and 30 min for us to reach Maldives from Cochin International airport. We had reached there at 10:30 AM, but then also it was quite hot. We took a taxi and went to the resort at Lankanfinolhu North. The taxi charged around 25 rf per km. The resort was near to the beach and we had a very good view of the sea from our room. Maldives is known for neat and clean white sand beaches, corals, sea diving, under water resorts, fishing and boating. Network is very poor in Maldives. By looking at the sea we could easily see the white sand beneath it. We had the underwater diving and it was full of miracle. Everything under the water was so pretty to see. But for some people the underwater diving would make the feel dizzy and vomit. The foriegn office warns that seas around the Maldives " can have strong tidal current " and that a number of people drawn each year. So the people who all are coming to the seas should always take local advise before entering to the water. As Maldives has many seas the majour occupation of the people living here is fishing. The two places in Maldives which you must go are 'Male and Addu Attol'. Maldives are absolutely perfect for the people who really love nature. I can't think of a better place for us to have spent this specific trip....
